01 Purpose

AI robots we can trust

AI robots must be dependable enough for people to entrust them with meaningful roles. That requires perceiving changing real-world environments, making context-aware decisions, and operating consistently while managing risk.

We represent states and tasks from visual observations and language instructions, then use human demonstrations to learn an initial policy through imitation learning.

We refine that policy with reinforcement learning and use a world model to predict skill-level state transitions. WMSO selects the next skill—and a safe way to hand off between skills—from a repertoire learned through imitation and reinforcement learning. By deploying this adaptive mechanism on the robot, we aim to build AI robots people can confidently rely on.

Learning matters when it works safely in the real world. Our reason to exist

02 Robotics

Perceive reality.
Master control.

01 / Vision

Machine visionPerceive the world

We estimate objects, poses, motion, and relationships from visual observations, then convert them into state representations for control.

02 / Language

Language groundingConnect meaning

We ground language instructions and task context in state representations, creating shared conditions across perception and action.

03 / Imitation

Imitation learningLearn from human

We learn an initial control policy from the actions and decisions contained in demonstration data.

04 / Reinforcement

Reinforcement learningImprove through trial

We optimize policies through rewards and environmental feedback, improving adaptation to conditions not seen during training.

05 / WMSO

World-model-based skill orchestrationOrchestrate for the situation

Using a world model, we treat skills learned through reinforcement and imitation learning as high-level options. As execution unfolds, the system reassesses the situation and integrates skill selection, transition, and recovery. Our goal is responsive skill switching as conditions change.

06 / Safety

Safety by designBuild in safety

It is not enough for an AI robot simply to move. We aim for risk-aware action selection in environments shared with people.
